History

The Coffee Shop That Started It All

Love Bug Coffee, nestled in the heart of Nashville, Tennessee, has a rich and vibrant history that spans several decades. From its humble beginnings to becoming a beloved coffee haven, this iconic coffee shop has played an integral role in the community and the city's coffee culture. Love Bug Coffee first opened its doors in 1975 when Sarah Jennings, a passionate coffee lover, embarked on a mission to bring the finest coffee experience to Nashville. Inspired by her love for both coffee and the city, she aimed to create a cozy and inviting space where locals and visitors could indulge in their caffeine cravings while fostering a sense of community. Initially, Love Bug Coffee started as a small, modest establishment, tucked away in a quaint corner of the city. Sarah believed in the importance of sourcing high-quality beans, and she traveled far and wide to establish relationships with reputable coffee growers. The shop quickly gained a reputation for serving exceptional coffee made from freshly roasted beans. As Love Bug Coffee grew in popularity, Sarah's daughter, Emily Jennings, joined her in running the business. Emily shared her mother's passion for coffee and introduced her own creative touch to the shop. Together, they expanded Love Bug Coffee, renovating the space to accommodate more patrons and creating a warm, welcoming atmosphere that became synonymous with the coffee shop. In the 1980s, Love Bug Coffee became a hub for artists, musicians, and creatives, reflecting Nashville's vibrant cultural scene. Local musicians would gather at the shop, strumming guitars and exchanging stories over cups of rich, aromatic brews. The walls were adorned with artwork and photographs, showcasing the talent that emerged from the city. Love Bug Coffee quickly became a beloved institution where creativity flourished. As Nashville's music industry continued to thrive, Love Bug Coffee found itself at the center of it all. Emerging musicians would often showcase their talents during intimate performances, creating an intimate and captivating atmosphere for both performers and the audience. Many successful musicians who got their start in Nashville have fond memories of Love Bug Coffee, recalling the shop as a source of inspiration and a place to connect with like-minded individuals.
